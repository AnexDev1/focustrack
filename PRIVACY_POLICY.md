# FocusTrack Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 10, 2026

## Summary

FocusTrack is a privacy-first desktop application. By default, FocusTrack stores your activity data locally on your device and does not transmit that data to FocusTrack-operated servers.

## What FocusTrack Collects

FocusTrack may collect and store the following information locally on your device so the application can function:

- Application names and window titles for the active foreground application.
- Session start times, end times, and duration values.
- Derived analytics such as category totals, focus scores, streaks, and usage summaries.
- User preferences required for app behavior and presentation.

## What FocusTrack Does Not Do

FocusTrack does not, by default:

- Require account creation.
- Upload your activity history to a FocusTrack server.
- Sell your data.
- Use third-party advertising SDKs.
- Track your keystrokes, clipboard contents, or typed text.

## Data Storage

Your data is stored locally on your device using app-managed local storage and a local SQLite database.

Typical storage locations include:

- Windows: `%APPDATA%\\focustrack\\`
- Linux: `~/.local/share/focustrack/`
- macOS: `~/Library/Application Support/focustrack/`

## Exports

If you choose to export data, FocusTrack can generate files such as JSON or CSV and save them to a location on your device chosen by the application workflow or your operating system.

## Your Choices

You can stop using FocusTrack at any time, uninstall it, and remove locally stored data by deleting the application data directory if desired.

## Children

FocusTrack is not directed to children under 13.

## Open Source And Self-Hosted Use

If you modify or redistribute FocusTrack, your version may behave differently. You are responsible for reviewing the source code and deployment behavior of any modified build.
